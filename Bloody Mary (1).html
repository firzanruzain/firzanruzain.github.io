Origin Story of Bloody Mary

Bloody Mary was the first-ever Queen of England to rule in her own right, but to her critics, Mary I of England has long been known only as "Bloody Mary"

This unfortunate nickname was thanks to her persecution of Protestant heretics , whom she burned at the stake in the hundreds. She was the bloodthirsty religious fanatic that posterity has bequeathed to us. While hundreds died under Mary’s reign, her dark legacy may have as much to do with the fact that she was a Catholic monarch succeeded by a Protestant Queen in a country that remained Protestant.

During her five-year reign, Mary had over 300 religious dissenters burned at the stake in what are known as the Marian persecutions . It is a statistic which seems barbaric. But her own father, Henry VIII , executed 81 people for heresy. And her half-sister, Elizabeth I , also executed scores of people for their faith.
